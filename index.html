Introduction

On Cabezon's third-substance reality:

The substance of the soul can materialise things in the physical world (eg. splitting of souls, ghost bodies, deformations, curses, immortalisation...) through time. Such power can come from the past, the future... (?)

Objects can exist in the past, the future, the present, or out of time - which is an abstract kind of place.

"The universe is spread out in four similar dimensions, which together make up a unified, four-dimensional manifold, appropriately called spacetime."

Substances

Please note that Cabezon's "substances" have nothing to do with drugs or alcohol.

First substance: the physical world. Described by science.

Second substance: The "soul" world. Concerned with consciousness. Some say that the second-substance categorization is moot (especially the practical Dons), however, it is conceptually necessary (especially considered to be by the logical Amans).

All soul matter and energy was created in the big bang, along with all physical matter and energy.

Third substance (AKA Catalysts):

  • the bridge between the First and Second substances.
  • Introduce a catalyst into the equation, and suddenly the Second Substance can impact the First Substance.
  • As souls are a type of Second Substance, Catalysts are a type of Third Substance (and the only one).
  • Catalysts can be split into 3 subcategories.

A quick digression on dreams: there are two types of dreams in Cabezon.

  • Soft dreams: are those that are truly just dreams- all animals have these dreams. They have no impact on anything.
  • Hard dreams: are significant, they occur in the realm Loulan where third substances can be formed. Ghosts can come into the dreams of dreaming agents, who are people who can have hard dreams. Any soul can come into a past dream through time travel, although what they can do in such a dream is limited.

There are also Property Locations. Locations which are infused with third-substance property. Example: the pool of Terracotta Cave. Unlike the dream world Loulan, however, these places are not second-substance worlds, because they are physically of the first-substance, they are only infused with a third-substance catalyst (Agent catalyst or Glitches). These have been placed by the Don Amans, way back when, during a war that was fought - these property locations were like trenches that served multiple purposes, such as allowing healing, rest, and crafting. These wars were a rehash, or an undoing, of previous wars.

FIRST SUBSTANCE can impact SECOND SUBSTANCE. SECOND SUBSTANCE cannot impact FIRST SUBSTANCE, unless through a catalyst.

Catalyst categories

Category 1 (Agent Catalyst): are used by agents.

  • Immortalizing soul: is a soul who can make the body it inhabitats immortal (with the use of an elixir).
  • Time travel: is possible in the realm of dreams or ghosts. (?)
  • Curses: Some curses bring out a quality in someone that they already had a predisposition to, but without the curse would not have come into fruition.
  • Rifle spells: Are a class of spells that make someone (the person the spell is placed on) "shoot" into the future or past in an instance of time, in one way - that can be touch, smell, emotion, sight, etc.

Category 2 (Base Catalyst): are not created by agents but affects them whether they like it or not.

  • Splitting of souls/alter egos: souls are, at their healthiest, one and whole. When a soul is seemingly split into multiple facets (for example, if an aspect of a person is repressed, then that repressed part may try and escape the body in search for its "own life"), the conception of an alter-ego might occur. Alter-egos do not have a physical body but can nonetheless appear in the world and interact with it. The person who the alter-ego splits from may not even know that this split has occurred. If an alter-ego is not reunited with its original body for a long enough period of time, it will begin to search for a body to inhabit (remember that being body-less is unnatural). This may culminate in the alter-ego seeking the original body and pushing the other soul out of the body, causing that soul to become the alter-ego without a body, and thus the cycle repeats. It may also seek out a different body entirely. Ideally, the two (or however many) parts should reconcile and live in the same body.
  • Deformations: one form of shapeshifting occurs when a soul is put into a body that it is either not ready for, or not made for- "wrong fitted". This causes the body (which involves both the mind, so mental, and physical body) to be deformed in order to "make space" for the soul. For example, Jonas' soul was not suited for a human body, thus resulting in "deformation of the mind" (also called "chaos in the brain"), in the form of a constant permeating anxiety. Another example is Lepus, who has a "deformation of the body" (also called "chaos in the body", being a giant (like, two adult humans tall) rabbit.
  • The Will of the Universe: Every universe has their own "Will". As far as the characters know, this "Will" is a person (and it is indeed, for their universe). All the Wills of the universes travel from universe to universe, but only in their own universe do they have some unique abilities (?).

Category 3 (Glitches): Or rather, "Glitschen" or "Glide" The nature of glitches can be altered by "zooming in and out" - making the glitches cover more ground (affect things to a bigger scale), or be miniscule (have only a small impact on things), and the frequency of the glitches occurrence. Glitches can be utilized by agents. Similarly to the naming scheme of substances, the 3rd category can be seen as a bridge or relation between the 1st and 2nd.

Souls

"Souls permeate through different stages of being. The reason for this is that souls have a body "in mind" that is suited for them, and it may take multiple instances to find the correct body, or to prepare to inhabit the proper body. Generally, the more fast physical movement that occurs for a body, the harder it is to inhabit, so it is most common for souls to inhabit celestial bodies at first before moving on to biological bodies." - the blueprint hypothesis

No new souls are made since the beginning of the universe. Souls never die, they are immortal. Souls themselves cannot die. The physical body can become ill, and the more ill it becomes the closer it gets to death. However, this is not the case with souls- a soul can become very ill, but it will never become ill to a point where the soul stops working.

The states a soul can be in:

  • in a body (living animal or celestial body)
  • or be a ghost. A ghost can be:
  • a wandering ghost, which most ghosts spend most of their time in,
  • or a figure ghost (such as Naomi and Lepus are) who take the form of something earthly.

The "Blueprint Hypothesis", theorised by the Don Amans.

The ideal form for a being is to be a soul in a body- and within this, to be in the body that the soul was created for (for example, a soul may be created with an animal species or type of celestial body in mind, and the soul may cycle through many different bodies before it finds the correct one). A soul being without a body (a ghost) is either a temporary state (such as wandering ghosts) in which it is seeking a new body. Once it has found the correct body and that body dies, the soul lies dormant with the body- so one day all the souls will become dormant.

Fitted-ness: When a soul has found their fitted body. This is their final "life". When the body dies, the soul lays dormant with the body.

Wrong fitted-ness: When a soul has not yet found their fitted body, they will be "wrong fitted" while inhabiting their current body. All souls will one day be fitted.

Digression: the concept of fittedness that the Don Amans have put forward is wrong.

  • Fittedness describes whether a soul's current body is their true, fitted body, that they will lie dormant with. The Don Amans came to this conclusion following observations and patterns.
  • The theory was dubbed "the Blueprint Hypothesis", as they theorised that a soul would have a "blueprint" for the ideal body.
  • They saw that a certain type of person would die and their soul would lie dormant. However, gaps began to appear: some people who did not fit the typical profile of a fitted soul would die and lie dormant, some people who fitted the profile did not lie dormant. This led them to the conclusion that although there is a profile of the typical fitted and unfitted soul, some people fall outside of it. However, in reality, it really meant that the concept of there being a distinction from the "fitted" and "unfitted" was flawed.
  • Lying dormant was hypothesized to be a state of "eudaimonia" - of calm, bliss, happiness. Since the distinction between the mind and the soul was less thought of back then, this concept made sense to them. In reality, it is true that the soul can experience eudaimonia while lying dormant in the body. Under the belief that the mind is a property of the body however, it might not make sense to say that the soul can experience anything after the body's death, since the mind would have died as well. The soul would then have no vessel through which to perceive anything. However, counterpoint, a soul can enter a celestial body and although its perception is not the same as an animals, it is a perception non the less- one that a celestial body itself does not have. It's the interpretation of perception, as well. A planet feels nothing, but the soul can perceive the nothing.
  • Example: Lepus. He has a dream as a wandering ghost - which is commonly conceptualised as a soul without a body. But it does have a body, just not a physical (first substance) body. What kind of body is it? A second substance body, for a second substance soul. It mocks (key term) the properties of a physical body, and serves the same function, but like magnets it repels its own and attracts and fits well with the other. Lepus also feels an affinity for rabbits. So a wandering ghost body can have feelings and desires, as well as some sort of drive. What kind of body is this? Is it somewhat like a second-substance weaved in the shape of a physical brain?
  • Where does this come from? How does the soul find this body? How was it created? Who created it? Well, it could only be the Don Amans - perhaps it was another type of Don Aman, a scientist - so not a Don or an Aman, but something else. An even more taboo kind of immortal.
  • Now before such brains were created, what would the natural course of the life of a soul be? Perhaps souls were intrinsically tied to bodies, a product of consciousness, and what the scientist does is separate all the "souls" from all the "bodies". Why?
  • One major flaw in the blueprint hypothesis is that all souls (second substance) are created along with physical matter (first substance) in the big bang, before "bodies" were a thing that the soul could "know" or have a blueprint for.
  • A different view may emerge: the Retroactive Hypothesis. That a soul has no intrinsic link or bond towards a body, but such a link may be formed.
  • The blueprint hypothesis states that all souls will lie dormant one day, except the immortal ones, who will live in their bodies forever.
  • The Don Amans, who are immortal, looked forward to this time. This also caused some of them to view the world in a somewhat flippant manner - leading to an attitude that is shared by many Don Amans. For example, Don Harpo wreaks so much havoc (even more than the average Don) and this would not be had he been raised in a different environment that valued non-immortal souls in a different light.
  • They also theorize that souls must learn pure evil and badness, as well as pure bliss and goodness. The "life" of a celestial body is pure good and the one of an animal pure evil. These two prepare the soul to inhabit a human body, which is a balance of the two.

    • Ghosts: What causes somebody to become a ghost? It happens when they die, but it doesn't happen to everybody. Ghosts of humans or animals occur when they are not ready to move onto the next stage. Their souls staying on Earth is purgatory. Sometimes, ghosts stay on Earth forever. For celestial bodies, becoming a ghost is a natural stage in their life- during this time they are wandering through the universe to find a place to reside after their bodies have been destroyed.

    In short: Soul in a celestial body -> wandering ghost (majority of time spent here) -> how many other bodies -> in their fitted body -> laying dormant with their fitted body

AARON

He seems like a depressed loner to most people. Has a passion for arts that has been stifled by the pressure to go into "real" studies. The crowd he was with chipped away at his resilience and motivation, leading him to screw up his senior year - he wans't where he wanted to be. They were hostile towards many facets of him. Feeling like he was hated in "secret" - as soon as he stepped out of hiding they would all turn on him and drop the facade of tolerance.

Compare this with Jonas, who also feels a disconnect with who he is and who others see him as (potentially a key theme of the story), and the subsequent punishment they both feel will happen if they reveal themselves for who they truly are.

Aaron is (was) in love with Chelsea, one of the only kind people in the same subjects as he. He latches onto her as the kind of person he wishes the others were like.

Aaron is, if not even more so, in love with art. He is deep into music, although he lacks the talent and drive to learn an instrument to a better standard. He picks up painting some time, too, representing a recovery from the mindset that shielded him from pursuing art, and his embracing of his passions.

The music that he likes reflects this too: during his first senior year he got really into electronic ambient music, because it was the type of thing he wouldn't ever want to make himself (although he enjoyed it). Listening to guitar-driven songs, he felt creative urges to make his own music, which he was trying to suppress. However, all his music tends to be slower and mellow.
